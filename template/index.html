What is depression?

  • Definition
  • Causes
  • Symptoms

Depression is one of the most common types of mental illnesses and often develops alongside anxiety. Depression can be mild and short lived or become severe and long lasting. Some people are only affected by depression once, while others can experience depression multiple times. Depression often leads to suicide, but this can be prevented with the right support. It is important to know that we can do many things to help young people who have suicidal thoughts.

What causes depression?
Depression can happen like a response to something usually traumatizing that happened. For example abuse, violence in school, passing of loved one or family violence. Anyone can be depressed after experiencing stress for long periods of time. Depression can also run in the family. Sometimes we don’t know clearly why depression happens.

Depression can bring different emotions to children. Here are some common signs and symptoms of depression:
Always feeling tired inside or always being in a state of tiredness, not having energy even after relaxing.
Always anxious or can’t focus on doing work.
Struggle to perform daily activities.
Feeling sad, worried or angry in a long period of time.
Always trying to be alone
